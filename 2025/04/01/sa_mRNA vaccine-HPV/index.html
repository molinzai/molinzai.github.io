Title: Single immunizations of self-amplifying or non-replicating mRNA-LNP vaccines control HPV-associated tumors in mice

IF: 17.1

Publication Type: Science translational medicine

PMID: 36867683

〇、文献背景

We generated lipid nanoparticle (LNP)–encapsulated self-amplifying mRNA as well as unmodified and nucleoside-modified non-replicating mRNA vaccines encoding a chimeric protein derived from the fusion of the HPV-16 E7 oncoprotein and the herpes simplex virus type 1 glycoprotein D (gDE7).

我们证实，用三种gDE7 mRNA疫苗中的任何一种进行单次低剂量免疫，都能诱导E7特异性CD8+ T细胞的活化，产生能够预防肿瘤复发的记忆性T细胞应答，并根除不同生长阶段的皮下肿瘤。
此外，gDE7 mRNA-LNP 疫苗还能在两种不同的小鼠正位肿瘤模型中，在注射单剂量疫苗后诱导有效的肿瘤保护。
最后，比较研究表明 三种 gDE7 mRNA-LNP 疫苗均优于 gDE7 DNA 疫苗和 gDE7 重组蛋白疫苗。
总之，我们在广泛的比较实验中证明了三种不同 mRNA 疫苗的免疫原性和治疗效果。的免疫原性和治疗效果。

4.3.1 Study design

The aim of this study was to assess the therapeutic antitumor efficacies of three different mRNA-LNP vaccine platforms, each encoding a hybrid HPV-16 E7 protein genetically fused to HSV-1 gD, based on an experimental murine model of HPV-16–induced tumors. We used nr-mRNA containing no modified nucleosides or N1-methylpseudouridine (m1Ψ)–containing nucleoside-modified mRNA. In addition, we designed an sa-mRNA formulation encoding the same target antigen. In select studies, the performance of the three gDE7 mRNA-LNP vaccines was compared to the immunogenicity and efficacy of gDE7 plasmid DNA (39, 40, 43, 44) and gDE7-purified recombinant protein vaccines (37, 42).

4.3.3 mRNA production
T7 RNA polymerase MEGAscript, Ambion
m1Ψ-5′-triphosphate TriLink
cellulose Sigma-Aldrich
2′-O-methyltransferase ScriptCap; CellScript

The gDE7 hybrid protein was designed on the basis of a codon-optimized sequence, as previously reported. The gDE7 (table S2) and firefly Luc coding sequences were cloned into optimized mRNA production plasmids to generate nr-mRNAs or samRNAs. mRNAs were produced using T7 RNA polymerase (MEGAscript, Ambion) on linearized plasmids. U sa-mRNA and U nr-mRNA were produced without the incorporation of modified nucleosides. m1Ψ-5′-triphosphate (TriLink) was used instead of uridine-5′-triphosphate to generate modified nucleoside-containing mRNA. mRNAs were purified by cellulose purification to remove double-stranded RNA contaminants, as previously described30049-6?_returnURL=https%3A%2F%2Flinkinghub.elsevier.com%2Fretrieve%2Fpii%2FS2162253119300496%3Fshowall%3Dtrue). mRNAs were capped using the m7G capping kit with 2′-O-methyltransferase to obtain cap1 mRNA final products (82). All mRNAs were analyzed by agarose gel electrophoresis and stored frozen at −20°C.

4.3.4 LNP formulation of mRNAs
Zetasizer Nano ZS (Malvern Instruments Ltd.)

mRNAs were encapsulated using a self-assembly process in which an aqueous solution of mRNA at acidic pH was mixed with an ethanolic lipid solution (83) of ionizable cationic lipids, phosphatidylcholine, cholesterol, and polyethylene glycol-lipids, similar to previously described compositions (83, 84). The ionizable cationic lipid and LNP composition are described in the patent application, WO 2017/004143. The size and polydispersity of the mRNA-loaded particles were characterized by dynamic light scattering using a Zetasizer Nano ZS (Malvern Instruments Ltd.). Encapsulation efficiency was determined using the RiboGreen assay. The mRNA-LNPs were subsequently stored at −80°C as previously described (83, 84).

DMEM Mediatech
FBS Corning
penicillin-streptomycin Gibco

HEK 293T cells: DMEM with high glucose + 10%FBS+P/S.

Human mo-DCs were generated from primary human monocytes cultured in RPMI 1640 (Gibco) containing 10% FBS and penicillin-streptomycin (50 U/ml), supplemented with recombinant human granulocyte-macrophage colony-stimulating factor (50 ng/ml) and IL-4 (100 ng/ml) cytokines (both from R&D Systems) every 3 days, and used on day 7.

TC-1 tumor cells were derived from the primary lung epithelial cells of C57BL/6 mice and transformed with c-Ha-ras and HPV-16 E6- and E7-encoding genes (85).

The TC-1–luc variant was obtained after transduction of TC-1 cells with a lentivirus-encoding firefly Luc (85). The cell line was cultured in RPMI 1640 supplemented with penicillin-streptomycin (50 U/ml), G418 (0.4 mg/ml; Sigma-Aldrich), 10% FBS at 37°C, and 5% carbon dioxide (CO2). The cells were routinely tested for the absence of mycoplasma by polymerase chain reaction. On the day of tumor grafting, the cells were treated with trypsin-EDTA (Gibco) and suspended in serum-free RPMI 1640 (Gibco) at appropriate concentrations.

4.3.7 Cell transfections

Luc为操作对照。

Transfection of HEK 293T cells or human mo-DCs was performed using TransIT-mRNA (Mirus Bio) according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Briefly, Luc or gDE7 mRNAs (0.1 μg) were combined with the TransIT-mRNA and Boost reagents in a serum-free medium, and the complex was added to 5 × 104 cells in a 96-well plate and cultured in complete DMEM (HEK 293T) or RPMI 1640 (mo-DCs). The in vitro luminescence experiments were carried out with cells lysed in Luc-specific lysis buffer (Promega) at different times after mRNA transfection. Aliquots were assayed for enzyme activity using the firefly Luc assay system (Promega), and luminescence was measured using a MiniLumat LB 9506 luminometer (Berthold/EG&G; Wallac). Alternatively, gDE7 and Luc mRNA-transfected cells were lysed for 30 min on ice in radioimmunoprecipitation assay buffer (Sigma-Aldrich) and collected for Western blotting analysis 24 hours after transfection.

4.3.8 Western blotting analyses
4× Laemmli buffer (Bio-Rad)

Whole-cell lysates from gDE7 mRNA-transfected cells were tested for the presence of the gDE7 protein. Samples were combined with 4× Laemmli buffer (Bio-Rad) and incubated at 95°C for 10 min, and then total protein was separated by size on 4 to 15% polyacrylamide gel using a Mini-PROTEAN TGX device (Bio-Rad) for 1 hour at 120 V. Transfer to polyvinylidene fluoride membranes was completed using a semidry transfer unit (Ellard Instrumentation) at 10 V for 1 hour. The membrane was blocked with 5% nonfat dry milk in Tris-buffered saline containing 0.1% Tween 20 (TBS-T). Protein was probed by incubating with a 1:10,000 dilution of 1D3 anti-gD mouse monoclonal antibody (provided by G. H. Cohen) or anti-glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase monoclonal antibody (clone 14C10 Rabbit) overnight at 4°C. Next, the membranes were incubated with donkey anti-mouse or anti-rabbit horseradish peroxidase–immunoglobulin G (1:5000; Jackson ImmunoResearch Laboratories) for 1 hour at room temperature. All antibodies were diluted in 5% nonfat dry milk in TBS-T. Blots were developed using the Amersham ECL Western Blotting Detection Reagent and an Amersham Imager 600 system (both from GE Healthcare).

4.3.9 In vivo bioluminescence imaging

Mice injected intramuscularly with Luc mRNA-LNP (3 μg/mouse) and those grafted with TC-1–luc cells were assessed for in vivo bioluminescence. At different time points, mice were intraperitoneally injected with 200 μl of D-luciferin (Regis Technologies) at a concentration of 150 mg/kg, and bioluminescence was observed using the IVIS Spectrum imaging system (Caliper Life Sciences, PerkinElmer), as previously described (86). The mice were anesthetized in a ventilated anesthesia chamber with 3% isoflurane in oxygen and imaged 5 min after D-luciferin injection. Bioluminescence was quantified in the region of interest to obtain the total flux values expressed in photons per second (p/s) using the Living IMAGE 4.0 Software provided by Caliper.

4.3.10 Tumor implantation

Subcutaneous tumors were induced after the injection of 1 × 105 TC-1 cells in 100 μl of PBS in the right dorsolateral region. Tumor growth was monitored two or three times a week with a caliper, and survival was recorded for at least 70 days. The smaller (d) and larger (D) diameters of the tumors were measured, and tumor areas were calculated according to the formula D × d. Mice were euthanized when the tumor size reached 15 mm in diameter or became necrotic. In some experiments, mice that remained tumorfree or cleared tumor after a primary challenge received a second tumor cell inoculation using 10 times more TC-1 cells (1 × 106 cells per mouse) 90 days after the first tumor challenge. For tumor cell infiltration assays, TC-1 cells were diluted in an extracellular matrix (Matrigel, BD Biosciences) before injection following the manufacturer’s instructions. Briefly, TC-1 cells, at a concentration of 1 × 106 cells per mouse, were resuspended in 100 μl of PBS, mixed with 100 μl of Matrigel at 4°C, and subcutaneously injected into the animals. Grafts of intravaginal tumors were achieved after female mice were diestrum-synchronized by a subcutaneous injection of medroxyprogesterone acetate (3 mg per mouse; DepoProvera) and, 4 days later, injected with 1 × 105 TC-1–luc cells per mouse into the submucosal layer at the lateral vaginal wall of the animal using 20 μl of cell suspension in PBS. Sublingual tumors were induced by injecting 5 × 104 TC-1–luc cells per mouse into the submucosa lining of the tongue diluted in 10 μl of PBS. Successful implantation of orthotopic tumors was confirmed with an IVIS Spectrum imaging system (Caliper Life Sciences) 3 days after injection, and animals were monitored for bioluminescence once a week. Mice were euthanized when the total flux reached 109 to 1010 p/s or when animals lost more than 20% of their body weight, according to animal care regulations.

4.3.11 Immunization regimens

The mRNA-LNP formulations were diluted in PBS and injected into the tibialis anterior muscle. Mice were immunized with a single intramuscular injection of 30 μg of gDE7-encoding mRNA-LNP to further confirm the potent immunogenicity and efficacy of the mRNA-LNPs. Negative control micewere injected with the same dose of nucleoside-modified Luc nr-mRNA-LNP. The vaccination schedule was determined on the basis of the growth curves according to our goals. For therapeutic tumor vaccine experiments, immunizations began 3 days after tumor engraftment, with a single injection of 30 or 5 μg of various gDE7 mRNA-LNP, unless otherwise indicated. Alternatively, tumor-bearing mice were immunized with decreasing amounts of mRNA-LNP to further assess the lowest dose capable of inducing antitumor responses. Immunizations were initiated when tumor sizes reached approximately 12 mm2 , usually on day 12 after the tumor cell graft. For the orthotopic tumor models, mice received therapy when the bioluminescence reached 106 or 107 p/s in the intravaginal and sublingual tumor models, respectively. To compare the different gDE7-encoding vaccine formats (mRNA-LNP, plasmid DNA, and recombinant protein), mice were vaccinated with a single dose of 5 μg of gDE7-encoding mRNA-LNPs (intramuscularly), with 30 μg of gDE7 recombinant purified protein (subcutaneously), or with 5 μg of gDE7 DNA vaccine by intramuscular injection followed by electroporation 10 days after the tumor implantation (9 mm2 ), and antitumor protection induced by the various vaccines was evaluated. The vaccine doses and routes of administration were chosen according to previous publications describing each formulation (37, 43). A total of six 130-V 450-ms electrical pulses were applied for electroporation using a NEPA21 Super Electroporator (NepaGeneCo), as described previously (44).

4.3.12 Tissue preparation

Single-cell suspensions of blood and spleen were prepared according to standard protocols previously described (23). Tumor samples were excised 6 days after tumor transplantation, cut into small pieces, incubated in PBS containing collagenase D (1 mg/ml; Roche) and deoxyribonuclease I (50 μg/ml; Roche), and incubated for 40 min at 37°C. The collagenase reaction was interrupted by the addition of 5 mM EDTA solution. The cell suspensions were filtered through 70- and 40-μm cell strainers (BD Biosciences) and rinsed with RPMI 1640 containing 10% FBS. Cells were centrifuged at 500g for 15 min and resuspended in PBS containing 2% FBS.

4.3.13 In vivo cytotoxic T lymphocyte assay

Single-cell splenocyte suspensions from naïve mice were divided into two aliquots and labeled with carboxyfluorescein diacetate succinimidyl ester (CFSE; Invitrogen) at final concentrations of 7 (CFSEhigh) or 0.7 μM (CFSElow). CFSEhigh cells were pulsed with CD8+ T cell-specific E7-derived peptide (2.5 μg/ml; 49RAHYNIVTF57; GenScript) in complete medium at 37°C for 40 min. Equal numbers of pulsed and nonpulsed cells from naïve mice were intravenously injected (a total of 2 × 107 cells per mouse) into mice intramuscularly immunized with 30 μg of gDE7 or Luc mRNA-LNP 14 days earlier. Splenocyte single-cell suspensions from immunized mice were prepared 18 hours later and analyzed for CFSE signal by flow cytometry using an LSR II flow cytometer (BD Biosciences). The numbers of CFSEhigh and CFSElow cells were used to calculate the percentage of peptide-pulsed target cell killing, determined using the following formula: 100 – 100 × [(%CFSEhigh gDE7-immunized/%CFSElow gDE7-immunized)/(%CFSEhigh Luc-injected/%CFSElow Luc-injected)].

4.3.17 Statistical analyses

Raw, individual-level data are presented in data file S1. Statistical analyses were performed using the GraphPad Prism software v.8.0. Multiple groups were compared using one- or two-way analysis of variance (ANOVA) with Bonferroni’s correction for multiple comparisons. Survival curves were compared using the log-rank (Mantel-Cox) test. Appropriate methods are indicated in the legends, with significant differences marked in all figures. Values and error bars are expressed as means ± SEM. Degrees of significance were defined as P < 0.05 (P < 0.05, P < 0.01, and P < 0.001). Figures were prepared using GraphPad Prism and Adobe Illustrator 2020 (Adobe Systems).
